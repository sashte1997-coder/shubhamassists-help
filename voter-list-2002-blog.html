If you have been asked to provide details from the 2002 Electoral Roll during the Special Intensive Revision (SIR) process, don't worry. You may be able to find the required information online without visiting the Election Office.

In this guide, I will explain how to search the official 2002 voter list and find details such as Part Number, Serial Number, Assembly Constituency, and EPIC Number.

What is the 2002 Electoral Roll?

The 2002 Electoral Roll is the official voter list prepared by the Election Commission. Archived voter rolls are available online for many states and can be useful for voter verification when required by election authorities.

Why Are Officials Asking for the 2002 Voter List?

During some verification processes under the Special Intensive Revision (SIR), officials may ask for the following information:

  • Father's or Mother's name
  • Assembly Constituency
  • Polling Station
  • Part Number
  • Serial Number (Sr. No.)

These details help verify historical voter registration records.

How to Search the 2002 Voter List Online

✓ Step 1

Visit the official Election Commission website and open the Search in Last SIR E-Roll page.

✓ Step 2

Select the following details:

  • State
  • District (as it existed in 2002)
  • Assembly Constituency (2002)
  • Polling Station

✓ Step 3

Enter the following information:

  • Elector Name
  • Approximate Age in 2002
  • Captcha

Click Search.

What Information Will You Get?

The search result may display the following details:

  • Elector Name
  • Relative's Name
  • Age
  • Assembly Constituency
  • Polling Station
  • Part Serial Number
  • EPIC Number (if available)

These are the details commonly requested during verification.

Tips if You Cannot Find the Record

If your name is not displayed in the search results, try the following:

  • Try different spellings of your name
  • Search using your father's or mother's name
  • Check another polling station if your village has multiple polling parts
  • Verify the district and constituency as they existed in 2002

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the 2002 voter list official?

Yes. The archived electoral rolls are published using official data received from the respective State Chief Electoral Officers.

What is a Part Number?

A Part Number identifies the polling part assigned to a polling station.

What is a Serial Number?

The Serial Number is your position within that polling part in the electoral roll.

Is the EPIC Number the same as the Serial Number?

No.

  • EPIC Number = Your Voter ID Number
  • Serial Number = Your position in the electoral roll

Conclusion

If election officials ask for information from the 2002 Electoral Roll, you can often locate it online using the official Election Commission records. Always use official government sources and follow the instructions given by the concerned election authorities.
